The capital of France is Paris.
Paris is one of the most visited cities in the world, known for its art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture.
It is situated on the Seine River, in the north of the country, at the heart of the Île-de-France region.
The city of Paris is the centre and seat of government of France and the Île-de-France administrative region.
Historically, the city has been a major center of finance, diplomacy, commerce, fashion, gastronomy, science, and arts.
For centuries, Paris has been one of the world's most important and attractive cities.
It is prized for the opportunities it offers for business and commerce, for study, for culture, and for entertainment.
The city contains numerous iconic landmarks, such as the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and Notre-Dame Cathedral.
The climate of Paris is classified as oceanic, with warm summers and cool winters.
The history of Paris dates back to the Parisii, a sub-tribe of the Celtic Senones.
By the 12th century, Paris was the largest city in the western world, a prosperous trading center.
It was the home of the University of Paris, one of the first universities in Europe.
During the Middle Ages, Paris was the largest city in Europe and an important religious and commercial center.
The city played a major role in the French Revolution in the late 18th century.
In the 19th century, it underwent a vast renovation under Napoleon III and Baron Haussmann.
Haussmann's renovation of Paris was a vast public works program commissioned by Emperor Napoléon III.
It included the demolition of medieval neighborhoods deemed overcrowded and unhealthy.
They were replaced by wide avenues, parks and squares, the annexation of the suburbs surrounding Paris, and the construction of new sewers.
Today, Paris remains a major global center for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture.
Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine.
Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral.
The city is known for its cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.
Paris is also the home of the most visited art museum in the world, the Louvre.
The Musée d'Orsay, noted for its collection of French Impressionist art, is also in Paris.
The Pompidou Centre Musée National d'Art Moderne, has the largest collection of modern and contemporary art in Europe.
Paris hosts the annual French Open Grand Slam tennis tournament on the red clay of Roland Garros.
The city is a major rail, highway, and air-transport hub.
Two international airports: Paris-Charles de Gaulle and Paris-Orly.
The Paris Métro is the city's subway system, opened in 1900.
It is one of the busiest metro systems in the world.
Paris is divided into 20 arrondissements (districts).
Each arrondissement has its own unique character and attractions.
From the historic Marais to the artistic Montmartre, there is something for everyone in Paris.
So, to summarize succinctly: The capital of France is Paris.
